Long-scrolling informational site

  • Go to site

Objective

Choosing any topic and any design style, create an informational site.

Implementation

I'd been seeing a lot of single-page long-scrolling sites being used for start-ups, designers, products... etc. and thought it would be a good fit. My wife was 7 months pregnant at the time, so we were talking a lot about education, and how we wanted to raise our child, so the topic of immersion education was an easy choice.

From a design side, I wanted to work with bold colors. I naturally am pretty conservative with colors, so I wanted to stretch myself that way.

I chose a system where each section of content would have its own color background, and re-used those colors for the text and graphics of the other sections to create some consistency and uniformity when the backgrounds are so different.

From a coding standpoint, it's a very simple site. Just one page with jump links on an nav fixed to the top of the page. I did learn how to create the full-width element with a nested centered element, which was very satisfying.

What I would do differently now

It's a static site, so I would make it responsive. I would also take a look at the nav and see if it makes more sense somewhere else. I think it's a little distracting on the top. Also given it's strctural simplicity, I could look at beefing up the content and adding more interactive elements like animations.
